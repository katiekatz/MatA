St. John Bosco Academy for Flippin’ Idjits

In 2015, the St. John Bosco Academy for Flippin’ Idjits was founded by three performers with extensive experience in numerous styles of dance, acrobatics, gymnastics, and martial arts with one goal in common: to prepare physical performers of all kinds to ply their craft in unconventional environments. With their different backgrounds and unique perspectives, they focus on giving students the groundwork needed to hone their skills through individual practice and hard work. Combined, they have over 23 years of teaching experience and a lifetime each of performing. Currently, they offer donation based seminars once a month in Harrington Park.

David Hugo

David Hugo has been a professor in the Theater Department at LIU CW Post since 2007. He teaches freshman acting and all the musical theater acting courses. David has performed regionally, on national tours and on Broadway in shows such as Jekyll and Hyde, Iolanthe, Into the Woods, and a stellar 12-year career with Les Miserables where he portrayed Grantaire in the final Broadway cast.
He was on tour with the Broadway production of Les Miserables when he developed a love for teaching through the workshops he gave at high schools and universities across America, Asia and Canada. He left the Les Miserables tour in 2005 to pursue a master’s degree in theater.
David holds an M.A. in Theater from LIU CW Post, a B.F.A. in acting from Syracuse University and an Associates Degree in Music from Onondaga Community College.

Alicia Rodis

Alicia Rodis is a certified teacher with the Society of American Fights Directors. Jeff Blumenkrantz

JEFF BLUMENKRANTZ has performed on Broadway in Bright Star, A Class Act, How to Succeed in Business… (1995), Damn Yankees (1994), Threepenny Opera (1989), and Into the Woods (1987), as well as Off-Broadway in Murder for Two (Lortel nomination), Forever Plaid, and in the City Center Encores productions of Anyone Can Whistle and God Bless You Mr. Rosewater. Regional theatre credits include productions at La Jolla Playhouse, Old Globe, Geffen Playhouse, Kennedy Center, Long Wharf, and Asolo. On camera, Jeff can be seen in The Big Sick, The Detour, 30 Rock, The Good Wife, Ugly Betty, Joseph … Dreamcoat, Will and Grace, Law and Order, and the PBS telecasts of the New York Philharmonic concerts of Sweeney Todd and Candide.
Also a composer, Jeff is a Best Original Score Tony® nominee (Urban Cowboy) and the recipient of the 2011 Fred Ebb Award. jeffblumenkrantz.com

Dave Milo

Dave Milo has been performing in theater for most of his life, beginning long ago at the Actors’ Cafe in Bradley Beach, New Jersey. He has spent the last nine years performing scenes, improv, and stage combat at the New York Renaissance Faire in roles such as Robin Hood, William Shakespeare, and a turnip farmer. In that time he has worked with and learned from countless individuals in the New York City professional theater community, from experienced Disney leaders to primetime television writers and fight masters in the Society of American Fight Directors. In 2013 he received his Master’s in Education at Hunter College, and has taught middle school and high school English in the Bronx and Harlem since. This year he has had the pleasure of adding Shakespeare and theater to his curriculum.

Tim Santos

Tim Santos received his early training in Akron, Ohio, and started his professional performing career dancing in productions for Cleveland Opera, the Houston Grand Opera, and with the Metropolitan Opera Ballet at New York’s Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts. He has appeared in productions at theaters throughout the United States including The Actor's Theater of Louisville, Houston's Theater Under the Stars, and Pittsburgh's Civic Light Opera. He was the assistant to Herbert Ross for Jubilee! at Carnegie Hall. Tim’s television credits include the ABC television remake of Bye Bye Birdie, the Super Bowl XXXIV half-time show and The Conan O’Brien Show.
In 1994 Tim started performing in the Christmas Spectacular at New York City's famed Radio City Music Hall. In 14 years at Radio City Tim’s responsibilities transitioned to include the positions of swing, dance captain, assistant choreographer and assistant director. He remained a member of the creative staff and a performing member of the Ensemble through the 75th Anniversary production of the Christmas Spectacular in 2007.
Tim developed his skills as an art’s educator, teacher and choreographer at the National Dance Institute (NDI) in the New York City Public Schools, under the leadership of Jacques d’Amboise. During that time he was the principal teacher/choreographer for the Borough of Brooklyn Outreach Program and also taught the S.W.A.T. and Celebration teams. He was the director of the Logan Summer Institute programs in 1993 and 1994 and directed Residency Programs in Trenton, NJ and Silver City, NM. More recently, Tim served as the Artistic Director of National Dance Institute of New Mexico (NDI-NM) from 2008-2010, overseeing quality art’s education for more than 6,200 students throughout the State of New Mexico each year.
Tim is currently the Director of Artistic Production at the Wyckoff Family YMCA, where he teaches dance at Studio 691, directs and choreographs for the Y Arts Theatre Company and the Y Arts Youth Theatre and contributes to all major public presentations at this thriving and dynamic organization.
Tim is always thrilled to be able to bring his love of musical theater and dance to a new generation of students. He is the Founding Artistic Director of the Broadway Theater Dance Workshop and has served on the Advisory Board of Rosie’s Theater Kids – an arts education program founded by Rosie O’Donnell, which brings a professional Broadway experience to 5th grade students in the New York City Public Schools. Tim is a presenter and faculty member at the annual Dance Teacher Web conference, bringing new and innovative concepts in dance, education and business to dance instructors, studio owners, college dance majors and professional dancers planning for career transition.
Tim has judged and taught for many national performing arts competitions and conventions. He is a member of Actor’s Equity Association, Screen Actors Guild, the American Federation of Television and Radio Artists, the American Guild of Musical Artists and the American Guild of Variety Artists.

Omar Edwards

Omar Edwards, award winning /tap dancer /entertainer/ musician, has been in love with tap dancing since he was twelve years old. Born in Brooklyn, New York, Omar’s dancing has been shaped by Jimmy Slyde, Marie Brooks and his legendary cousin, Savion Glover. Omar, first earned national acclaim when he and tap partner Daniel B. Wooten Jr, were grand prize-winners on the famed TV show “Star Search”. From there Omar embarked on a career that now spans the worlds of music, dance, television, theatre, and film. His journey includes performances in a variety of venues, including a stellar performance as a featured dancer in Bring in 'da Noise, Bring in 'da Funk on Broadway; in concert at the Whitehouse with Savion Glover, for President Barack Obama; an appearance on the Ellen DeGeneres Show and as a featured dancer in "Live at the Hollywood Bowl" with Alicia Keys. Professional theatre audiences recently saw Omar as the Tap Griot in FLY, an Award winning play about the famed Tuskegee Airmen of WWII, by playwrights Trey Ellis and Ricardo Khan. His Performance in FLY garnered a BESSIE Award nomination at the New Victory Theatre/New York, and a BroadwayWorld.com/LA Award nomination for best performance by a featured actor at the Pasadena Playhouse.
