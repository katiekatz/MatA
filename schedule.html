The Physicality of Theatre

The Physicality of Theater is a workshop designed to strengthen an actor's awareness of their body and its capabilities for storytelling. Participants will practice altering their posture, movement patterns, and expressions beyond their comfort zones in order to facilitate a complete transformation. Activities will include full-body stretching, silent storytelling and communication, movement and stride training, and others designed to extend an actor's range and confidence in their body. Comfortable clothing is needed, as the workshop will consist of many movement based exercises.

Understanding Shakespeare

Watch your thoughts. They become words. Watch your words. They become deeds. Watch your deeds. They become your character. Much like this idiom, Shakespeare's characters live in a world where thought, word and deed are intricately connected. For actors deciphering these worlds and characters it is helpful to think of this idiom (somewhat) in reverse. Watch your words, they become thoughts. Watch your thoughts, they become deeds. Watch your deeds, they become your character. We will spend some time looking at Shakespeare's text and exploring some tools to help us tease apart a soliloquy in search of a character.

Acrobatics

This fast-paced class will give students a basic introduction to acrobatics, dance, and stunt work. It will be geared towards all skill levels; no prior experience is necessary, and intermediate and advanced students are welcome to take the opportunity to refine their technique. We'll begin with a warm-up, before moving on to basic rolls, cartwheels, and breakfalls. Techniques and skills can be adapted and altered to better fit the specific needs and aptitudes of each student.

Story Through Song

Almost all of us have sung "Happy Birthday to You" more than a few times in our lives. Every birthday, around a cake with candles, we make our way through that simple, familiar melody. Why haven't we all grown tired of this tradition and moved on already?
Because it's not about the song. It's about the person we're celebrating.
STORY THROUGH SONG explores ways to make your singing specific, dynamic and unique to you. Bring sheet music of a song you love with you to this seminar. We'll work together to unlock its potential and make it yours.

Directing

What makes Shakespeare's plays "universal"? Why do we still come back to them? In this class we will begin to explore how to stage Shakespeare's text by employing keen script analysis, object use, and placing actor's in space. By watching clips from past productions and conducting our own examination of his text, we will begin to formate what each of us sees and feels in his words, and how to translate those feelings into dynamic staging.

The Art of Voiceover

This master class will give students an opportunity to learn about the world of voiceovers from a leading industry professional. Debra will begin class with a group warm up, after which each student will choose and record a piece of voiceover copy. Debra will then individually coach each student, helping to bring out their own personal strengths. Students will learn about microphone technique, vocal dexterity, and how to make the copy their own. Everyone will have an opportunity to hear their work played back after their coaching session is complete. This class is perfect for anyone who is curious about voiceovers and wants to have some fun behind the mic!
